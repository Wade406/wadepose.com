Ideas Made Physical

Taking abstract concepts and giving them material form

Abstract concepts require material presence to become real. Working primarily in clay, I develop forms that embody psychological states, temporal processes, and contemplative practices. Each piece emerges from a specific conceptual challenge — how do you give physical presence to memory, transformation, or interior experience?

Missing

Ceramic, Slip Cast Fabric, Steel, Wood, Textile, 144" x 48" x 4" (2010)
An installation exploring absence and memory through accumulated forms. Individual elements create a unified field that suggests both fragmentation and cohesion.

Gallery Installation

Various works, ceramic and mixed media (2009-2010)
Professional exhibition context showing range of sculptural approaches, from intimate torso studies to architectural interventions.

Acidrainlady

Ceramic, Multiple High Fire, 42" x 16" x 15" (2009)
Geometric intervention in organic form. The relationship between structure and surface becomes a meditation on urban transformation.

Communications

Ceramic, Mishima, 37" x 15" x 14" (2009)
Surface as language. Intricate linear patterns create a visual syntax that suggests both circuit boards and neural networks.

Process Documentation

Collaborative large-scale work, unfired ceramic
Working at architectural scale requires both individual focus and collaborative coordination. The practice becomes as important as the product.

This represents a small selection of sculptural investigations from 2009-2010. The work continues to evolve, but the core question remains consistent: how do we make the invisible visible through material engagement?
