Preface to the Libretto

On the Nature of This Libretto

The 42nd Threshold is offered not as a finalized performance plan, but as a conceptual field document—a writing sample representing the kind of integrative, ritual-based media art I intend to develop during my time in the MFA program.

This piece is speculative, flexible, and porous by design. It is a scripted artifact that behaves more like a waveform than a blueprint. I expect the final work, if and when it is realized, to evolve dramatically based on:

  • Ongoing research into biofeedback, performance, and frequency entrainment
  • Dialogue and collaboration with faculty, cohort members, and visiting artists
  • Constraints of space, time, budget, and institutional support
  • Technological discovery and real-time audience feedback

In this sense, the libretto functions not as a proposal, but as a proof-of-intent—a demonstration of my current conceptual vocabulary and performative trajectory. It allows you to glimpse how I think through time, silence, presence, and ritual structure.

This project is also an invitation to co-shape what it may become.

On Audience, Aftermath, and Archiving

While The 42nd Threshold describes a 42-hour performance ritual, its true architecture extends beyond the temporal container of the piece. I view the performance not as a singular event but as a generative field—one that includes:

  • Audience curation and invitation as part of the ritual design (including outreach to figures like Marina Abramović and local Montana communities)
  • Time-slotted witnessing to create a slow, reverent influx of participants rather than a spectacle-driven crowd
  • Post-performance reintegration, including:
    • A filmed documentation process
    • A public Q&A or panel, held days later, as the formal completion of the 42nd threshold
    • A ritual "after-party" where reflection and resonance may be shared collectively

Even the marketing, in this framework, becomes art: not promotion but signal. The intention is to cast waves before the event even begins, so that those who arrive are already in tune with the field.

In practice, this project will unfold across three years of development, beginning with scaled performance trials (6, 12, and 24 hours) and culminating in the full 42-hour event. Planning will include venue coordination, safety protocol development, technical system testing, and timeline refinement. Audience engagement will involve local and national outreach to artists, contemplatives, and donors, along with curated invitations. Practical logistics will include organizing discounted lodging for out-of-town participants, coordinating volunteers and acolytes, and catering the post-performance gathering. The afterparty, documentary process, and public Q&A are not peripheral events but integrated thresholds of the work itself.

This is not simply a performance. It is a constructed mythos. And this document is not simply a script. It is the invocation.

The 42nd Threshold — Libretto

I. INVOCATION

This piece unfolds across 42 hours. One hour per threshold. Each hour a passage of vibration, silence, and presence. This is not a play. It is a field.

(You may leave at any time. But something will stay with you.)

I expect my creation to be the manifestation of entrainment.

My art is not content. It is a carrier wave.

I am not speaking to you. I am vibrating near you.

What you feel in the bones of your ears, the wet hollows of your mind—
That is the piece.

I do not ask you to understand.
I ask you to arrive.

This text is not written. It is cast.
You are not reading it. You are being shaped by it.

Note: The language here draws from incantation and ritual structures, used intentionally as aesthetic method, not metaphysical claim.

You may not be the same after this.
This performance is designed to provoke internal shift.
If you enter of your own free will, with full knowledge and consent,
Know that you may be changed.

This is not the beginning. This is the opening.
This is Threshold 1.

II. FIELD (Instructions for Entry)

Construct a room.
Smoke-stained walls. Low sound.
Black floors. Copper breath.

Dim passageways channel the audience into place.
Elaborate tapestries or panels line the walls.
Light from above. Narrow beams.
A hushed reverence sets in.

The performer enters wearing a robe.
The acolytes begin the preparation.

One attaches the EEG. Another, the EKG.
Galvanic skin monitors. Pulse sensors.
Another brings in the small table.
On it: tea, an incense burner, a cloth folded in fourths.

The Attendant enters and stands behind the performer.
They carry the Staff of Compassion — a short, dark staff, copper-capped, held loosely but deliberately.

They do not speak. They do not move unless needed.
They will change out every two hours, without ceremony, but with reverence.
Their role is not to direct, but to guard the edge of the field.

Then the chant begins.
It starts strong.
One syllable per breath.
Perhaps:

*Om namah shivaya hum.*

The sonic arc unfolds in phases:

  • The first 8 hours are carried by the performer's external voice — strong, vocalized, projecting into the room.
  • Gradually, looped samples of the chant begin to build — feedback layered upon itself — until the room begins to fill with the voice folded in time.
  • The performer transitions into a whisper voice. The texture becomes thinner, but the loops remain loud.
  • Filters in the space begin to modulate the chant — removing highs, reinforcing lows — until the text of the chant becomes unintelligible. What remains is waveform.
  • As the performer transitions to internal voice, he no longer contributes to the audible sonics.
  • The chant echoes continue, then begin to decay.
  • By Threshold 39, only environmental resonance remains. The chant has become memory.
(Sub-bass drone begins.)
(High-pass filters begin to narrow the audible field.)

The room starts to vanish.
Only the light remains.
Only the sound.

A whisper from the audience: "Something shifted."

Then I disappear.
Then they disappear.
Only waveform and resonance remain.

(Performer's EEG, EKG, GSR, and breath data are being visualized live in another space.)

At Threshold 3, the acolyte moves casually.
At Threshold 9, he slows.
By Threshold 19, he walks barefoot.
At Threshold 27, he lays the bowl with both hands.
At Threshold 37, he enters like a shrine.

The audience breathes together.
Some shuffle quietly out—carrying something heavy.
They do not know what it is.
Only a few remain.
Perhaps only one.

[Who stays for 42 hours? It's not a requirement. But if someone does—let it be enough.]

III. BACK CHANNEL

EEG channels mapped to visual distortion.
EKG pulses converted into sub-bass.
Skin conductance triggers the emergence of light.

The performer is disappearing.
You don't see it at first.
His edges fray. His limbs phase.
The body becomes frequency.
The chant becomes geometry.

They have reached the 19th threshold.
The audience does not know.
They only know they are floating.

The chant no longer describes.
It has become vibration.
Then whisper.
Then silence.

But the waveforms continue.
Transducers hum beneath the floor.
The room continues to pulse.
The breath of the ritual echoes even when no sound is made.

IV. ASCENT

(whispered)

What is this?
A sculpture?
A song?
A spell?

No.

It is a grimoire you do not carry.
It lives inside you now.

The body begins to reappear.
The chant is gone.
But the transducers still hum.

Threshold 41: The geometry collapses.
The acolyte approaches one last time.
He does not move quickly.
He bows without instruction.
The field is holding.

Now it is silent.
But the waveform remains.
The room still pulses.

There is only one person left.
An audience of one.
A new day dawns.

This is Threshold 41.

And now, silence.
A page.
A breath.
A line waiting to be written:

Threshold 42: ____________________________________________

Scientific Context and Media Architecture

Performance Installation Concept: Liminal Waveforms Vigil

This concept sheet outlines the proposed MFA thesis performance/installation project. The work integrates endurance-based ritual, scientific visualization, and audience participation to explore altered states of consciousness as both subject and medium. The intention is to create a rigorously grounded yet transformative experience that demonstrates the relevance of Media Arts to consciousness studies, neuroscience, and performance art traditions.

Core Project Elements

1. Endurance Ritual – A 42-hour chanting performance, documented live, modeled after long-durational works by artists such as Marina Abramović. This establishes the human/ritual anchor.

2. Scientific Visualization Layer – AI-generated fractal/animated film depicting the proposed neural pathway of meditation and sensory gating (sodium/potassium gates, thalamic filtering, cortical feedback). This merges scientific fidelity with artistic abstraction.

3. Human Anchor (EEG Icon) – A small inset display of the performer (with EEG leads visible) grounds the work in lived reality, ensuring the audience knows the data has a biological basis.

4. Contradictory Overlay – Dense scientific narration (ions, channels, networks) paired with psychedelic visuals. The mismatch between language and imagery is deliberate: it destabilizes cognitive processing, creating a threshold state in the audience.

5. Soundtrack / Sonic Entrainment – Layered chanting, low-frequency sound, and EEG-driven sonification disrupt ordinary perception, entraining the audience toward altered states.

Artistic and Theoretical Context

This project sits at the intersection of performance art, media installation, and consciousness research. It draws lineage from Abramović (presence/endurance), James Turrell (light as medium), Bill Viola (video as meditation), and Rafael Lozano-Hemmer (biofeedback installations). Unlike these precedents, however, the piece actively integrates granular neuroscientific models of meditation (presynaptic inhibition, thalamic gating, DMN suppression) to frame the performance in both artistic and scientific registers.

Intended Impact

The audience is not only informed but physiologically and psychologically engaged. By forcing the brain into a state of addled contradiction—watching scientific explanation, witnessing human ritual, and hearing destabilizing sound—the work creates the conditions for the audience to experience an altered state themselves. This positions the installation as both demonstration and induction of consciousness modulation.

Conclusion

The Liminal Waveforms Vigil is not a traditional MFA thesis but a boundary-crossing project. It demonstrates how Media Arts can provide space for the exploration of consciousness through hybrid practices that are part art, part science, and part ritual. The project does not ask faculty to provide technical expertise in neuroscience—it only asks for an academic container where the work can unfold and be witnessed.
